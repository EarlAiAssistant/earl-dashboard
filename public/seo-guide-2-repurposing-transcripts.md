## Part 3: Repurposing Playbooks (By Transcript Type)

### Playbook #1: Customer Interview → Content Engine

**Source:** 30-60 minute customer call

**Repurposing workflow (2-3 hours):**

**1. Blog Post (60 minutes)**
- Structure: Challenge → Solution → Results
- Title: "How [Company] [Achieved Outcome] with [Your Product]"
- Include 3-5 direct quotes from interview
- Add metrics (% increase, hours saved, revenue impact)
- SEO optimize for "[Industry] + [Problem]"

**2. Case Study PDF (45 minutes)**
- 2-page format
- Company background
- Challenge (quantify the pain)
- Solution (how they use you)
- Results (with customer quote)
- CTA (Try free trial)

**3. Email Sequence (30 minutes)**
Email 1: Introduce the problem (their challenge)  
Email 2: Make it worse (agitate the pain)  
Email 3: Present solution (your product)  
Email 4: Social proof (customer quote + results)  
Email 5: CTA (trial or demo)

**4. Social Media (20 minutes)**
- LinkedIn: Customer quote + mini-story
- Twitter: 7-tweet thread of key insights
- Instagram: Quote graphic + before/after

**5. Sales Assets (15 minutes)**
- Add to objection-handling playbook
- Update competitive battle card
- Create "customer spotlight" slide for decks

**Total time: 2 hours 50 minutes**  
**Output: 15-20 pieces of content**

### Playbook #2: Expert Interview → Thought Leadership

**Source:** Podcast episode, webinar, expert roundtable

**Repurposing workflow (3-4 hours):**

**1. Main Blog Post (90 minutes)**
- Title: "[Number] Lessons from [Expert Name] on [Topic]"
- Structure: List-style or Q&A format
- Pull 5-7 key insights
- Quote expert liberally
- Link to full podcast/video

**2. Mini Blog Series (60 minutes)**
- Turn each key insight into a 500-word post
- Example: "Why [Expert] Says [Controversial Take] About [Topic]"
- Publish 1 per week

**3. LinkedIn Article (30 minutes)**
- "What I Learned from [Expert]"
- Personal reflection + expert quotes
- 800-1,200 words

**4. Social Amplification (45 minutes)**
- Create 10-15 quote graphics
- Write 7-tweet thread
- 5 LinkedIn posts (one key takeaway each)
- Tag the expert (increases reach)

**5. Email Newsletter (20 minutes)**
- Subject: "3 surprising insights from [Expert]"
- Include audio clip or video snippet
- Link to full episode

**6. Video Clips (30 minutes)**
- Pull 3-5 best moments (30-90 seconds each)
- Add captions
- Post as YouTube Shorts, TikTok, Reels

**Total time: 3 hours 50 minutes**  
**Output: 25-30 pieces of content**

### Playbook #3: Sales Call → Sales Enablement

**Source:** Discovery call, demo, competitive conversation

**Repurposing workflow (1-2 hours):**

**1. Objection Handling Script (30 minutes)**
- Document the objection raised
- Note how you handled it
- Extract best response
- Add to sales playbook

Example:
> Objection: "You're more expensive than [Competitor]"  
> Response: "You're right—we're 30% more expensive. Here's why customers pay more: [3 reasons]. One customer said: '[Quote]'. Would you rather save 30% upfront or 10x your ROI?"

**2. Battle Card Update (20 minutes)**
- Competitor mentioned? Add to battle card
- Note why prospect considered them
- Document why they chose you (or didn't)
- Share with sales team

**3. Use Case Documentation (15 minutes)**
- Prospect shared unexpected use case?
- Document it
- Add to "Creative Ways Customers Use [Product]" doc
- Share with product/marketing teams

**4. Demo Talking Track (20 minutes)**
- What demo moments resonated?
- Which features got "wow" reactions?
- What questions came up?
- Update demo script

**5. Social Proof (15 minutes)**
- If they converted: Ask for testimonial
- Pull quote from call ("Your demo showed me exactly what I needed")
- Add to website testimonials

**Total time: 1 hour 40 minutes**  
**Output: 5+ sales assets**

### Playbook #4: Webinar → Educational Content Machine

**Source:** 60-minute webinar or presentation

**Repurposing workflow (4-5 hours):**

**1. Main Blog Post (90 minutes)**
- Title: "Everything We Covered in [Webinar Name]"
- Embed video at top
- Summarize key points (5-7 sections)
- Add screenshots from slides
- Include Q&A

**2. Slide Deck → LinkedIn Carousel (30 minutes)**
- Pull 7-10 key slides
- Reformat for LinkedIn carousel
- Add captions
- Post with summary in caption

**3. Video Clips (60 minutes)**
- Cut into 5-7 short segments
- Each covers one key point
- Upload to YouTube (full playlist)
- Share segments on LinkedIn, Twitter

**4. Email Series (45 minutes)**
- "Missed our webinar? Here's what you missed"
- Break into 3-4 emails
- Email 1: Overview + link to recording
- Email 2: Key insight #1
- Email 3: Key insight #2
- Email 4: Q&A highlights + next webinar

**5. Infographic (60 minutes)**
- Visualize key stats from webinar
- Example: "5 Stats Every [Role] Should Know About [Topic]"
- Post on LinkedIn, Pinterest, embed in blog

**6. Podcast Episode (20 minutes)**
- Extract audio from webinar
- Minimal editing
- Publish as podcast episode
- Cross-promote

**Total time: 4 hours 20 minutes**  
**Output: 20-25 pieces of content**

## Part 5: Advanced Repurposing Strategies

### Strategy #1: The Content Library

Don't repurpose one-off. Build a system.

**Create a content library:**
- Folder 1: Raw transcripts
- Folder 2: Extracted quotes (tagged by theme)
- Folder 3: Published content (blog, social, email)
- Folder 4: Templates (blog outlines, email structures)

**Tag everything:**
- #customer-story
- #objection-handling
- #competitive
- #product-feedback
- #thought-leadership

**Benefit:** When you need content on [topic], search your library. Instant research.

### Strategy #2: Thematic Repurposing

Instead of repurposing one transcript at a time, batch by theme.

**Example:**
- Interview 5 customers about pricing objections
- Repurpose all 5 into:
  - Blog series: "5 Pricing Objections (And How We Handle Them)"
  - Email sequence targeting price-sensitive leads
  - Sales enablement: "Pricing Objection Playbook"

**Result:** Deeper, more authoritative content.

### Strategy #3: Seasonal Refresh

Old transcripts ≠ dead content.

**Every 6 months:**
- Re-read old customer interviews
- Pull new quotes for updated case studies
- Create "best of" compilations
- Update blog posts with recent data

**Example:**
2024 interview: "We saved 10 hours/week"  
2026 update: "We now save 15 hours/week and added 2 new team members"

### Strategy #4: The Collaborative Transcript

Interview two people at once:
- Customer + Account Manager
- Two customers in same industry
- You + Industry Expert

**Why?** Dialogue is more engaging than monologue.

**Repurposing gold:**
- Blog: "A Conversation Between [Person A] and [Person B] About [Topic]"
- Podcast: Already recorded, minimal editing
- Social: Pull debate-style quotes ("Person A says X, Person B says Y")

## Part 6: Common Mistakes

### Mistake #1: Not Recording Calls

"I'll just take notes."

You'll forget 80% of the good stuff.

**Fix:** Record every customer-facing call. Ask permission: "Mind if I record this for my notes?"

### Mistake #2: Waiting Too Long

Transcript sits for 2 weeks. Context is lost.

**Fix:** Block 2 hours immediately after the call to repurpose.

### Mistake #3: Over-Editing

Making customer quotes "perfect" kills authenticity.

"Um, so, like, I was super frustrated before."  
→ Don't change to: "I was frustrated before."  
→ Change to: "I was super frustrated before."

**Fix:** Remove filler, keep voice.

### Mistake #4: Ignoring Failed Calls

Lost deal? Churned customer? Still valuable.

**Use cases:**
- "Why we lost [Customer] to [Competitor]" (internal post-mortem)
- Objection handling playbook
- Product roadmap priorities

**Fix:** Repurpose negative outcomes too (internally).

### Mistake #5: No Distribution Plan

You created 20 pieces of content. Now what?

**Fix:** Create a distribution schedule:
- Week 1: Publish blog post, email to list
- Week 2: LinkedIn article, share blog
- Weeks 3-6: Drip social media posts
- Month 2: Refresh and re-share
