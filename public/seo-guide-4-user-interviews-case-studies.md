## Part 1: Why User Interviews Make the Best Case Studies

### The Traditional Approach (Doesn't Work)

Most companies create case studies like this:
1. Ask customer for testimonial
2. Customer writes 2 sentences
3. Marketing tries to expand it into a case study
4. Result: Generic, boring, unconvincing

### The Interview Approach (Works)

Here's what happens when you interview customers instead:
1. Book 30-minute call
2. Ask the right questions
3. Record the conversation
4. Extract quotes, metrics, stories
5. Write case study in their voice

**Result:** Authentic, detailed, persuasive.

**Why it's better:**
✅ **Customers are more candid** in conversation than in writing  
✅ **You control the narrative** (ask questions that surface the best stories)  
✅ **Richer detail** (30-minute interview = 4,500 words of raw material)  
✅ **Authentic voice** (use their actual words, not corporate speak)

## Part 2: The Interview Framework

### Step 1: Who to Interview

**Best candidates:**
- **Power users** - High engagement, long tenure, vocal advocates
- **Recent wins** - Just achieved measurable results
- **Diverse industries** - Show breadth of use cases
- **Churned → returned customers** - Great "before/after" story

**Red flags:**
- Customers who only use basic features (weak story)
- Recent signups (no results yet)
- Customers with NDA restrictions (can't publish)

### Step 2: The Outreach Email

**Subject:** Quick favor: 15-minute call about your [Product] experience?

Hi [Name],

I've noticed you're getting great results with [Product]—[specific metric or observation].

I'd love to feature your story in a case study to show other [industry] companies what's possible.

**What's in it for you:**
- Backlink to your website (SEO boost)
- Social proof for your company
- Exposure to our audience
- Optional: 3-month upgrade or $500 gift card

If you're open to it, I just need 20-30 minutes to chat about:
- How you're using [Product]
- What results you're seeing
- What advice you'd give others

Grab a time here: [Calendar link]

Thanks!  
[Your name]

**Conversion rate:** ~60-70% (if they're happy customers)

### Step 3: The Interview Questions

**Section 1: Before State (10 minutes)**

"Let's start before you used [Product]. Walk me through what your workflow looked like."

**Follow-ups:**
- "What specific problem triggered you to start looking for a solution?"
- "What were you using before? Why wasn't it working?"
- "Can you quantify the pain? Time wasted, revenue lost, etc."

**Goal:** Paint a vivid picture of the "before" state. The worse it was, the better the case study.

---

**Section 2: Decision Process (5 minutes)**

"How did you find us?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "What other products did you evaluate?"
- "What almost made you choose a competitor?"
- "What convinced you to choose us instead?"
- "If you had to sell [Product] to a colleague, what would you say?"

**Goal:** Understand why they chose you (this becomes your positioning).

---

**Section 3: Implementation (3 minutes)**

"Walk me through getting started. What was that experience like?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "How long did it take to see value?"
- "Any surprises during setup?"
- "What would you tell someone getting started today?"

**Goal:** De-risk the buying process ("It was easier than I expected").

---

**Section 4: Results (10 minutes) ⭐ MOST IMPORTANT

"What's changed since you started using [Product]?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "Can you quantify the impact? Time saved, revenue increased, etc."
- "What would happen if you couldn't use [Product] anymore?"
- "Any unexpected wins or use cases?"
- "How has this impacted your team/customers/business?"

**Goal:** Get hard numbers and emotional reactions.

**Example answers you want:**
- "We went from 10 hours/week to 2 hours/week on reports."
- "We closed 30% more deals because we had data to back up our proposals."
- "Honestly, I'd have to hire someone if we lost this tool."

---

**Section 5: Advice (2 minutes)**

"If you were talking to someone considering [Product], what would you tell them?"

**Goal:** Pull quote for the case study ("Don't overthink it—just try it").

## Part 3: The Case Study Structure

### Template: The Classic 4-Part Case Study

**Part 1: Company Background (2-3 sentences)**

"[Company] is a [size] [industry] company based in [location]. They serve [customer type] by [what they do]."

**Example:**
> "CloudSync is a 50-person SaaS company based in Austin, Texas. They provide analytics software for marketing teams at mid-market B2B companies."

---

**Part 2: The Challenge (2-3 paragraphs)**

Start with the pain. Make it specific.

**Structure:**
- What was broken?
- What did they try before?
- Cost of the problem (time, money, frustration)

**Example:**
> "Before using [Product], CloudSync's product team spent 10+ hours per week manually exporting analytics data to Google Sheets. This created several problems:
> 
> First, reports were always outdated. By the time Sarah (PM) finished compiling data, it was already 2-3 days old.
> 
> Second, the manual work meant fewer reports. The team could only publish 2 reports per month, when they needed 8-10 to make informed decisions.
> 
> They tried Tableau and Looker, but both required a data engineer to set up. As a lean startup, CloudSync couldn't justify the hire."

**Key:** Use their actual words from the interview.

---

**Part 3: The Solution (2-3 paragraphs)**

How did they use your product to solve the problem?

**Structure:**
- How they discovered you
- What convinced them to try
- Implementation process
- Key features they use

**Example:**
> "Sarah discovered [Product] through a Product Hunt launch. What caught her eye was the promise of 'automated reporting without a data team.'
> 
> She signed up for the free trial skeptically—'I've been burned before by tools that promise automation'—but within 10 minutes, she had her first automated report running.
> 
> The team now uses [Product]'s automated CSV exports to pull data directly into their dashboards. Every Monday morning, the latest metrics are waiting in Slack. No manual work required."

---

**Part 4: The Results (3-4 paragraphs)**

This is the payoff. Make it quantifiable and emotional.

**Structure:**
- Primary metric (time saved, revenue increased, etc.)
- Secondary wins (unexpected benefits)
- Direct quotes from customer
- Future plans

**Example:**
> "The impact was immediate. What used to take 10 hours now takes 2 hours—a **80% time savings**.
> 
> But the time savings weren't the biggest win. Sarah's team now publishes 8-10 reports per month instead of 2. This means faster iteration and better decisions.
> 
> 'It's like having a data analyst on the team, but I don't have to hire one,' Sarah said. 'We're making better product decisions because we actually have data to back them up.'
> 
> CloudSync also discovered an unexpected use case: their marketing team started using [Product] to pull campaign data. This wasn't the original intent, but now marketing is a power user too.
> 
> Looking ahead, Sarah plans to expand usage to the sales team next quarter."

---

**Part 5: Call-to-Action**

End with a clear CTA.

**Example:**
> "Want results like CloudSync? Try [Product] free for 14 days—no credit card required.
> 
> [CTA Button]"

## Part 4: From Interview to Published Case Study

### Timeline: 2-3 Hours

**Hour 1: Interview (30 min) + Transcription (30 min)**
- Conduct interview
- Get transcription (automated is fine)
- Quick skim to identify best quotes

**Hour 2: Writing (60 min)**
- Draft case study using template
- Pull quotes from transcript
- Add metrics and specifics
- Write headline and intro

**Hour 3: Review & Publish (30-60 min)**
- Send draft to customer for approval
- Make edits based on feedback
- Design PDF (use Canva template)
- Publish on website

**Total:** 2-3 hours from interview to published case study.

## Part 5: Advanced Strategies

### Strategy #1: The Video Case Study

Same interview, but on video.

**Why?**
- More engaging than text
- Can pull clips for social media
- Build trust (seeing real people)

**How:**
- Ask: "Can we record video for this?"
- Use Zoom, Riverside.fm, or SquadCast
- Edit highlights (3-5 minutes)
- Embed in case study page

**Pro tip:** Offer customer a free video for THEIR marketing in exchange for permission to use clips.

---

### Strategy #2: The Multi-Customer Case Study

Interview 3-5 customers in the same industry.

**Example:** "How 5 SaaS Companies Reduced Churn by 30% Using [Product]"

**Why?**
- Shows pattern (not one-off success)
- Addresses "Will this work for ME?" objection
- One blog post = 5 testimonials

---

### Strategy #3: The Before/After Case Study

Interview customer twice:
- **Interview 1:** Right after they sign up (capture "before" state)
- **Interview 2:** 90 days later (capture "after" results)

**Why?**
- More accurate metrics (not relying on memory)
- Build long-term relationship
- Show transformation over time

---

### Strategy #4: The Competitive Win

Interview customers who switched from a competitor.

**Key questions:**
- "What were you using before?"
- "Why did you switch?"
- "What's better about [Product]?"

**Use case:** Competitive battle cards, comparison pages, sales enablement.

## Part 6: Common Mistakes

### Mistake #1: Asking for Testimonials Instead of Interviews

"Can you send me a testimonial?"

→ You get 2 sentences.

**Fix:** Book a call. Extract gold through conversation.

---

### Mistake #2: Writing in Corporate Speak

"[Company] leveraged [Product] to optimize operational efficiency."

❌ Boring. No one talks like this.

**Fix:** Use customer's actual words.

> "[Product] saved our team 10 hours a week. Now we actually have time to do our jobs instead of fighting with spreadsheets."

---

### Mistake #3: No Metrics

"[Company] saw great results with [Product]."

❌ What does "great" mean?

**Fix:** Quantify everything.
- Time saved: "10 hours/week → 2 hours/week"
- Revenue impact: "+30% deal close rate"
- Growth: "2 reports/month → 8 reports/month"

---

### Mistake #4: Waiting Too Long

Interview → 2 weeks pass → Context is lost.

**Fix:** Write the case study within 48 hours while the conversation is fresh.

---

### Mistake #5: Not Getting Customer Approval

You publish a case study. Customer gets mad. Legal gets involved.

**Fix:** Always send draft for approval before publishing. Include:
- "Can we use your name/company?"
- "Can we share these metrics publicly?"
- "Any edits you'd like?"
