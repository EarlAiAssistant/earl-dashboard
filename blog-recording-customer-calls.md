## Is It Legal to Record Customer Calls?

**Short answer:** Yes, if you get consent.

**Long answer:** It depends on where you and your customer are located.

### **US: One-Party vs. Two-Party Consent**

**One-party consent states (38 states):**
- Only **one person** needs to know the call is being recorded (you)
- You can record without telling the other person

**Two-party consent states (12 states):**
- **All parties** must consent to being recorded
- You must notify the customer and get permission

**Two-party states:**
- California
- Connecticut
- Florida
- Illinois
- Maryland
- Massachusetts
- Michigan
- Montana
- Nevada
- New Hampshire
- Pennsylvania
- Washington

**Best practice:** Always ask for consent, regardless of state laws. It's the ethical thing to do, and it builds trust.

---

### **EU/UK: GDPR Compliance**

If your customer is in the EU or UK, you must:
- ✅ Get **explicit consent** (not implied)
- ✅ Explain **why** you're recording
- ✅ Explain **how** the recording will be used
- ✅ Allow them to **opt out**
- ✅ Store recordings securely (encryption)
- ✅ Delete recordings after a reasonable period (unless they consent to long-term storage)

**Example GDPR-compliant consent script:**
> "I'd like to record this call so I can accurately reference what we discuss for a potential case study. The recording will only be used internally and won't be shared publicly without your approval. Is that okay with you?"

---

### **Canada: Similar to US Two-Party**

- One party must consent (you), **but**...
- If you plan to **share or publish** the recording, you need explicit consent from all parties

**Best practice:** Treat it like GDPR — get clear, documented consent.

---

### **Australia: One-Party Consent (Usually)**

- Generally, one-party consent is fine
- Exception: If the recording is for illegal or improper purposes

**Best practice:** Still ask. It's just polite.

## How to Ask for Permission (Without Being Awkward)

Most people overthink this. Here's how to ask naturally.

### **Option 1: Simple and Direct**

> "I'd like to record this call so I can focus on our conversation instead of taking notes. Is that okay?"

**Why it works:**
- Clear reason ("so I can focus")
- Low pressure (just asking, not demanding)
- Most people say yes

---

### **Option 2: Transparency About Use Case**

> "I'd like to record this for two reasons: (1) so I don't miss anything you say, and (2) in case we turn this into a case study later. Sound good?"

**Why it works:**
- Sets expectations up front
- Gives them agency ("Sound good?" not "I'm recording.")
- Shows you value their input

---

### **Option 3: GDPR-Compliant (EU/UK)**

> "For GDPR compliance, I need to ask: Is it okay if I record this call? It'll only be used internally for notes and potentially a case study if you approve it later. You can say no, and we'll just take notes instead."

**Why it works:**
- Legally compliant
- Gives them an out ("You can say no")
- Builds trust

---

### **What If They Say No?**

**Don't panic.** Just say:

> "No problem! I'll take notes instead. Thanks for letting me know."

Then:
- Take detailed notes during the call
- Immediately after, write down key quotes while they're fresh
- Send a follow-up email: "Hey, just to confirm — you said [quote]. Is that accurate?"

## Best Practices for Recording Customer Calls

### **1. Test Before the Call**

**Do a test recording 5 minutes before:**
- Check audio levels
- Verify transcription is working
- Make sure you have enough storage

**Pro tip:** Record a 30-second test ("Testing, one two three") and play it back. Saves you from "Oh no, it didn't record" moments.

---

### **2. Start Recording at the Beginning**

**Don't wait 10 minutes to ask.**

**Bad:**
> [10 minutes into call] "Oh, by the way, can I record this?"

**Good:**
> [Before you start] "Quick question before we dive in — is it okay if I record this?"

**Why:** Starting mid-call makes it awkward and you lose the first 10 minutes.

---

### **3. Have a Backup**

**Always record with two methods:**
- Primary: Zoom cloud recording
- Backup: Otter.ai or local OBS recording

**Why:** If one fails (WiFi drops, service crashes), you still have the conversation.

---

### **4. Store Recordings Securely**

**Where to store:**
- ✅ Encrypted cloud storage (Dropbox, Google Drive with encryption)
- ✅ Password-protected folders
- ❌ Public links (never!)
- ❌ Unencrypted email attachments

**GDPR compliance:**
- Delete recordings after 30-90 days (unless customer consents to long-term storage)
- Don't share with third parties without explicit consent

---

### **5. Label Clearly**

**Use a consistent naming convention:**

```
YYYY-MM-DD_CustomerName_CallType.mp3
```

**Example:**
```
2026-01-31_Sarah-Chen_SuccessInterview.mp3
```

**Why:** You'll thank yourself in 6 months when you need to find that one quote.

---

### **6. Transcribe Immediately**

**Don't let recordings pile up.**

**After every call:**
1. Download the transcript (Zoom, Otter, Fireflies)
2. Scan for quotable moments
3. Highlight 5-10 key quotes
4. Save to a "Quotes" doc

**Pro tip:** Do this within 24 hours. After that, you'll forget the context.

---

### **7. Get Consent to Publish**

**Recording ≠ Publishing**

Just because they let you record doesn't mean they consented to:
- Publishing quotes in a case study
- Using their name in marketing
- Sharing video clips on social media

**Before publishing, ask:**
> "Hey [Name], I'd love to feature your story in a case study. Would you be okay with us using [quote] and mentioning [Company Name]?"

**If they say no:** Anonymize it. "A SaaS founder we work with saved 20 hours/week..." (no name, no company)

## Common Mistakes (And How to Fix Them)

### ❌ **Mistake #1: Not Testing**

**What happens:** You finish the call and discover it didn't record.

**Fix:** Test 5 minutes before every call.

---

### ❌ **Mistake #2: Only Using One Tool**

**What happens:** The tool crashes mid-call. You lose everything.

**Fix:** Always have a backup recording method.

---

### ❌ **Mistake #3: Forgetting to Hit "Record"**

**What happens:** 30 minutes in, you realize you forgot to click the button.

**Fix:** Add "Start recording" to your pre-call checklist.

---

### ❌ **Mistake #4: Not Asking Permission**

**What happens:** Customer feels ambushed. Trust is broken.

**Fix:** Always ask up front.

---

### ❌ **Mistake #5: Assuming Consent = Publication Rights**

**What happens:** You publish a case study. Customer is pissed because they didn't agree to that.

**Fix:** Get separate consent for publication.
