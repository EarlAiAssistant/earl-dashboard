## The 12 Content Types (And How to Create Them)

### 1. Case Study (800-1,200 words)

**What it is:** A detailed success story showing how the customer used your product to achieve results.

**Structure:**
- Headline: "How [Customer] [Result]"
- Customer intro (who they are)
- The challenge (before using your product)
- The solution (how they use your product)
- The results (specific metrics)
- Pull quote

**Where to use it:**
- Dedicated case studies page on your website
- Sales proposals
- Email attachments

**Time to create:**
- Manual: 2-3 hours
- With AI: 10-15 minutes

**Example:**
> **How Sarah's Agency Saved 15 Hours a Week and Closed $180k in New Business**
>
> Sarah Martinez runs a solo marketing agency with 8 clients. Before using ReportFlow, she spent 15 hours every week manually creating client reports...
>
> [Full story with metrics and quotes]

### 6. Sales One-Pager

**What it is:** Single-page PDF summarizing the customer's success story, optimized for sales proposals.

**Structure:**
- Company logo + customer logo
- Headline: "How [Customer] [Result]"
- Challenge (3 bullet points)
- Solution (3 bullet points)
- Results (3 metrics with icons)
- Quote (large pull quote)
- CTA: "See how [Product] can help you achieve similar results"

**Where to use it:**
- Attach to sales proposals
- Send in follow-up emails
- Print for in-person meetings
- Add to sales deck

**Time to create:** 20 minutes (in Canva or Google Docs)

### 7. FAQ Section

**What it is:** Common questions the customer asked during the interview, turned into FAQ entries.

**How to extract:**
- Scan transcript for customer questions
- Group by theme (pricing, features, implementation, etc.)
- Write clear answers based on what you told them

**Example:**
```
Q: How long does implementation take?
A: Most customers are up and running in less than a week. Sarah had her first automated report generated within 2 days of signing up.

Q: Do I need technical skills?
A: No. Sarah described it as "surprisingly easy‚Äîif you can upload a file, you can use it."

Q: What if I have custom reporting needs?
A: You can customize templates to match your brand and data sources. Sarah uses custom branding for each of her 8 clients.
```

**Where to use it:**
- Product landing pages
- Pricing page
- Knowledge base
- Sales enablement docs

**Time to create:** 20 minutes

### 9. Pain Points Summary

**What it is:** Distilled list of challenges the customer faced (useful for messaging, sales, product).

**How to extract:**
- Scan transcript for phrases like "struggled with," "frustrated by," "wasted time on"
- Group into categories
- Prioritize by frequency/intensity

**Example:**
```
PAIN POINTS (from Sarah's interview):

Time drains:
- 15 hours/week on manual reporting
- Friday afternoons completely consumed
- No time for business development

Process friction:
- Data scattered across 5 tools
- Manual copy-paste workflow
- Formatting inconsistencies

Emotional impact:
- "Soul-crushing work"
- Mental exhaustion by 6 PM Fridays
- Resentment toward necessary but low-value tasks
```

**Where to use it:**
- Marketing messaging
- Website copy
- Sales scripts
- Product roadmap prioritization

**Time to create:** 10 minutes

### 10. Objections + Responses

**What it is:** Questions/concerns the customer had before buying, plus how you addressed them.

**How to extract:**
- Look for phrases like "I was worried about," "I almost didn't sign up because," "My concern was"
- Document your response (or what convinced them)

**Example:**
```
OBJECTION: "I'd tried automation tools before and they never worked."
RESPONSE: Free trial with demo transcript (instant value, no risk)
OUTCOME: "I uploaded my first client's data and 10 minutes later had a report that would've taken me 2 hours."

OBJECTION: "Seems too expensive for a solo consultant."
RESPONSE: ROI calculator showing time saved = money saved
OUTCOME: "The time I save is worth $8,000/month. I'm paying $47. It's not even close."
```

**Where to use it:**
- Sales training
- Website copy (FAQ section)
- Email sequences
- Pricing page

**Time to create:** 15 minutes

---

### 11. Feature Requests

**What it is:** Ideas the customer mentioned for product improvements.

**How to extract:**
- Scan for "I wish you had," "It would be cool if," "Have you thought about"
- Document with context (why they want it, use case)

**Example:**
```
FEATURE REQUEST: Automated scheduling (send reports Monday AM)
CUSTOMER: Sarah
USE CASE: "I still have to remember to generate reports. If they sent automatically every Monday, I could literally never think about it."
PRIORITY: High (directly reduces manual work to zero)

FEATURE REQUEST: Client-facing portal
CUSTOMER: Sarah
USE CASE: "My clients ask 'where's the latest report?' It'd be great if they could just log in and see it."
PRIORITY: Medium (nice-to-have, not critical)
```

**Where to use it:**
- Product roadmap planning
- Engineering prioritization
- Customer feedback database

**Time to create:** 10 minutes

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Creating Only One Thing
**Wrong:** Interview ‚Üí case study ‚Üí done  
**Right:** Interview ‚Üí 12 assets ‚Üí full month of content

**Fix:** Use this checklist. Extract every ounce of value.

---

### Mistake 2: Not Planning the Calendar
**Wrong:** Create everything at once, publish randomly  
**Right:** Space it out over 4 weeks

**Fix:** Schedule in advance. One interview = one month.

---

### Mistake 3: Generic Repurposing
**Wrong:** Same quote on LinkedIn, Twitter, Instagram (no customization)  
**Right:** Optimize for each platform

**Fix:**
- LinkedIn: Professional tone, longer posts
- Twitter: Conversational, thread format
- Instagram: Visual-first, quote graphics

---

### Mistake 4: Not Tracking Performance
**Wrong:** Publish and forget  
**Right:** Track what works, double down

**Fix:** Monitor:
- Which case studies get the most traffic?
- Which LinkedIn posts get engagement?
- Which emails get replies?

Do more of what works.
