## How to Get Great Testimonials

### 1. Ask Specific Questions
**Don't ask:** "Can you write a testimonial?"

**Do ask:**
- "What problem were you facing before using [Product]?"
- "How much time/money has it saved you?"
- "What would you tell a colleague about [Product]?"

### 2. Pull From Customer Interviews
The best testimonials come from actual conversations, not forms.

**During interview:**
- Listen for quotable moments
- Ask follow-up: "Can I quote you on that?"
- Get permission to use their name/company

### 3. Offer to Draft It
**Email template:**
> "Hey [Name], would you be comfortable with me using this as a testimonial?
>
> '[Quote from your interview]'
>
> Let me know if you'd like me to adjust anything!"

Most people say yes. Saves them time.

## Testimonial Checklist

**Every great testimonial should have:**
- [ ] Specific numbers (time, money, percentages)
- [ ] Before/after context
- [ ] Customer's real name + title
- [ ] Sounds conversational (not corporate-speak)
- [ ] Addresses a pain point or objection
- [ ] Short enough to read in 10 seconds

**Red flags (avoid these):**
- [ ] Vague language ("great product," "highly recommend")
- [ ] No attribution (anonymous)
- [ ] Too long (>5 sentences)
- [ ] Sounds fake/scripted
