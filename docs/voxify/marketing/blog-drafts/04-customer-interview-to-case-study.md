# Customer Interview to Case Study: A 5-Step Framework

*The fastest path from "great conversation" to "published case study."*

You just had a call with a happy customer. They shared results, told stories, and said things that would make amazing marketing material.

Now you need to turn that conversation into a case study.

## The 5-Step Framework

### Step 1: Capture the Right Moments

Not everything in a customer interview belongs in a case study. You're listening for specific types of moments:

**The "Before" Moments**
- What was life like before they found your solution?
- What pain were they experiencing?
- What had they tried that didn't work?

*Listen for:* Frustration, struggle, wasted time/money, emotional stakes

**The "Decision" Moments**
- Why did they choose you over alternatives?
- What almost stopped them from signing up?
- Who else was involved in the decision?

*Listen for:* Objections overcome, differentiators, buying triggers

**The "After" Moments**
- What changed after implementing your solution?
- What specific results did they achieve?
- How do they feel now compared to before?

*Listen for:* Numbers, time saved, money made, emotional relief

**The "Quotable" Moments**
- Any sentence that would work as a headline
- Colorful language or memorable phrases
- Authentic enthusiasm

*Listen for:* Energy shifts, laughter, "I love that..."

### Step 2: Extract Key Elements

From your interview, pull out these specific components:

**Customer Context**
- Company name, size, industry
- Their role and responsibilities
- Why they're a good example (similar to your target customer)

**The Problem (1-2 paragraphs)**
- Specific pain point they experienced
- Impact on their work/business
- What made it urgent to solve

**The Search (1 paragraph)**
- How they found you
- What alternatives they considered
- Why they chose you

**The Solution (2-3 paragraphs)**
- How they implemented your product
- Which features they use most
- Any unexpected benefits

**The Results (1-2 paragraphs)**
- Quantifiable outcomes (time, money, efficiency)
- Qualitative improvements (stress, confidence, capability)
- Specific examples or anecdotes

**The Headline Quote**
- One sentence that captures the transformation
- Should work standalone in an ad or social post

### Step 3: Structure for Conversion

Case studies that convert follow a specific structure:

```
HEADLINE
[Result-focused title or customer quote]

SNAPSHOT
- Company: [Name]
- Industry: [Industry]
- Challenge: [One-line problem]
- Results: [Key metric]

THE CHALLENGE
[2-3 paragraphs: Their situation before you]

THE SOLUTION
[2-3 paragraphs: How they implemented your product]

THE RESULTS
[2-3 paragraphs: What changed, with specific numbers]

KEY QUOTE
[Pull quote, prominently displayed]

CALL TO ACTION
[What you want readers to do next]
```

**Why this structure works:**
- Headline and snapshot let skimmers get the gist
- Challenge creates relatability ("That's my problem too!")
- Solution shows your product in action
- Results provide proof
- Quote adds authenticity
- CTA captures interest

### Step 4: Write to Convert

A few principles that make case studies more effective:

**Lead with results in the headline**

❌ "How Acme Corp Uses Voxify"
✅ "How Acme Corp Cut Content Creation Time by 80%"

**Use their exact words**

❌ "The customer expressed satisfaction with the product's efficiency."
✅ "'I went from spending 4 hours to spending 20 minutes. It's ridiculous how much time I've saved.' – Sarah, Marketing Director"

**Be specific, not vague**

❌ "They saw significant improvement in their workflow."
✅ "Within the first month, they published 12 blog posts—compared to 2 the month before."

**Include one obstacle overcome**

Show that it wasn't magic—they had to do something, overcome a hurdle, or change a habit. This makes the story believable and actionable.

**End with a clear CTA**

❌ "Contact us to learn more."
✅ "See how you can get similar results. Start your free trial →"

### Step 5: Get Approval and Publish

Before publishing, run through this checklist:

**Customer Approval**
- [ ] Customer reviewed and approved the case study
- [ ] They're okay with company name being used (or agreed to anonymize)
- [ ] Quote is accurate and they're comfortable with it
- [ ] Any numbers have been verified

**Content Check**
- [ ] Results are specific, not vague
- [ ] Structure follows the framework
- [ ] Reads in under 5 minutes
- [ ] CTA is clear and actionable

**Distribution Prep**
- [ ] Featured image or graphic created
- [ ] Social posts drafted for promotion
- [ ] Customer tagged in social posts (if they're okay with it)
- [ ] Added to case studies page on website

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Mistake #1: Waiting too long**

Strike while the conversation is fresh. Within 48 hours of the call, you should have a draft. Wait longer and you'll forget context, lose momentum, and the customer might forget details too.

**Mistake #2: Making it about you**

The customer is the hero, not your product. They had a problem, they solved it, they got results. Your product is the tool they used, not the star of the show.

**Mistake #3: Vague results**

"Significant improvement" means nothing. "45% reduction in response time" means everything. Push for specific numbers in the interview.

**Mistake #4: Over-polishing**

Done is better than perfect. A case study that's 80% as good but published today beats a perfect case study that never gets finished.

**Mistake #5: No distribution plan**

A case study on your website does nothing if nobody sees it. Plan the promotion: social posts, email to prospects, sales team enablement.

After the interview:
- [ ] Transcript created within 24 hours
- [ ] Key moments highlighted
- [ ] Draft written using the 5-part structure
- [ ] Customer approved the final version
- [ ] Published and promoted
