## Why Customers Don't Give Testimonials (And How to Fix It)

**The problem:** You email 20 customers asking for testimonials. You get 2 responses.

**Why this happens:**

### Reason 1: You're Asking Too Much
"Can you write a testimonial?" sounds like homework.

**Fix:** Make it easy. Draft it for them based on what they've already said.

### Reason 2: They Don't Know What to Say
Blank page = paralysis.

**Fix:** Ask specific questions instead of asking for a "testimonial."

### Reason 3: No Immediate Benefit to Them
"Help us with marketing" isn't compelling.

**Fix:** Offer something in return (exposure, discount, feature request priority).

### Reason 4: Bad Timing
Asking 1 day after signup? Too soon. 6 months later? They forgot.

**Fix:** Ask right after a "wow" moment.

## How to Write Testimonial Requests

**Bad request:**
> "Hey, can you write us a testimonial?"

**Good request:**
> "Hey [Name], I noticed you [specific action]. Would you be comfortable sharing what [Product] has helped you achieve? Even 1-2 sentences would be amazing. Happy to draft something based on your feedback if that's easier!"

**What makes it good:**
- Personal (noticed specific action)
- Easy (1-2 sentences)
- Helpful offer (draft for them)

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Asking Too Early
**Wrong:** Ask 1 day after signup  
**Right:** Wait 30-60 days (or until "wow" moment)

### Mistake 2: Generic Request
**Wrong:** "Can you write us a testimonial?"  
**Right:** "What results have you achieved with [Product]?"

### Mistake 3: No Incentive
**Wrong:** "Help us with marketing!"  
**Right:** "Share your story, get 3 months free"

### Mistake 4: Making It Hard
**Wrong:** "Fill out this 20-question form"  
**Right:** "Reply with 2 sentences, I'll format it"

### Mistake 5: Not Following Up
**Wrong:** Send one email, give up  
**Right:** Follow up after 3 days if no response
