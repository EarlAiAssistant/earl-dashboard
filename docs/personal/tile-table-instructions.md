# Tile Table Building Instructions

**Project:** DIY Ceramic Tile Table with Wood Trim | **Location:** Indoor use | **Date:** January 30, 2026

---

## Materials List

### Hardware & Fasteners
- **2" finish nails, 16-gauge steel** (~$3-5 per box) - perfect for trim work
- Hammer, nail set, wood putty

### Tile Supplies
- Ceramic tiles
- Construction adhesive (tubes)
- Premixed grout (sanded for gaps >1/8", unsanded for smaller)
- Grout float, sponges

### Wood Finishing
- **Stain:** 8 oz (half pint) - Minwax or Varathane (~$5-8)
- **Polyurethane:** Water-based satin/semi-gloss quart (~$12-18)
  - **Top picks:** Minwax Polycrylic or Varathane Water-Based
- **Grout sealer:** Penetrating type (~$12-20)
  - **Best:** Aqua Mix Sealer's Choice Gold or Miracle 511 Impregnator
- Foam brush, sandpaper (120-220 grit)

---

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### 1. Stain & Attach Wood Trim (Day 1-3)

**Prepare trim BEFORE attaching (easier!):**
1. Cut trim to fit around plywood perimeter
2. Apply stain, let dry 24 hours
3. Apply 3 coats water-based poly (sand lightly between coats with 220-grit)
   - Each coat dries 2-4 hours
   - This prevents grout staining later!

**Attach trim to plywood:**
1. Position trim flush or slightly proud of plywood surface (tiles should NOT sit higher than trim)
2. Pre-drill pilot holes near ends to prevent splitting
3. Nail with 16-gauge steel finish nails every 8-10 inches, angled slightly inward
4. Countersink with nail set, fill holes with wood putty, sand smooth
5. Touch up poly over filled holes

### 2. Plan & Adhere Tiles (Day 4-6)

**Layout:**
- Dry-fit tiles first to plan pattern
- Leave 1/16"-1/8" gap between tiles and trim (prevents cracking)

**Adhere:**
1. Apply construction adhesive in zigzag pattern on plywood
2. Press tiles firmly with slight twist
3. Wipe off squeezed adhesive immediately
4. **WAIT 24-48 hours before grouting** (check adhesive tube for cure time)

### 3. Grout (Day 6-7)

1. Remove spacers, scoop premixed grout onto tiles
2. Spread with grout float at 45¬∞ angle, work diagonally
3. Scrape excess with float at 90¬∞
4. **Wipe tiles with damp sponge while grout is wet** (circular motions, rinse sponge often)
5. Let haze dry 15-30 min, buff with dry cloth

### 4. Seal Grout (Day 14)

**CRITICAL - Wait 7 days for grout to cure!**

1. Apply penetrating grout sealer (Aqua Mix or Miracle 511)
2. Use foam brush or spray, let soak 5-10 minutes
3. Wipe excess off tiles
4. Let cure 24 hours before using table
5. Prevents stains from drinks/food

**Why these choices:**
- **16-gauge steel nails:** Perfect balance - thin enough not to split, strong enough to hold
- **Water-based poly:** Dries in 2-4 hours (vs 8-24 for oil), doesn't yellow, easy cleanup
- **Penetrating sealer:** Soaks into grout (not topical coating), looks natural, lasts longer

## Troubleshooting

**Trim splitting:** Pre-drill pilot holes, avoid nailing within 1" of ends  
**Tiles shifting during grouting:** Adhesive not cured - remove grout, re-adhere, wait 24h+  
**Grout staining wood:** Should've sealed first - try mineral spirits, sand/refinish if needed  
**Grout cracking:** Normal hairline cracks happen - seal anyway (fills small cracks)
